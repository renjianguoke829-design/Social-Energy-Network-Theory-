Epistemological positioning: This is a historical philosophy with physical constraints as hard boundaries — not a physics theory, not a historical narrative. II. Basic Concepts

2.1 Social Energy

Social energy is both the motive force and the product of consciousness-matter interaction motion — the two form a closed self-organizing loop, not a linear causal relation. Social energy has qualitative gradations; high-grade social energy can directly drive forward motion, while low-grade energy requires extensive processing before it can be utilized.

Three forms of social energy: flow form (currently produced available energy in real time); organizational potential form (ordered structure fixed in organizations, institutions, network connections); material crystallization form (low-entropy arrangements fixed in infrastructure, tools, cultural products). Maintaining ordered structure requires continuous work to resist entropy increase; stopping work leads to spontaneous structural degradation.

EROI (Energy Return on Investment): extractable available energy ÷ input energy. When EROI falls below the minimum threshold for maintaining social complexity, the system physically fails regardless of institutional form.

Available energy: the net effective energy a system can actually use to maintain order and resist entropy increase — not the total quantity of all energy forms.

2.2 Energy Illusion (能量幻象)

Any credential or signal that can circulate within a system and be used by nodes as if it were actual energy for decision-making, but systematically diverges from actual available energy. Criterion: trace actual energy flows (electricity consumption, freight volume, physical output) — not credential flows. A persistently widening divergence between the two is the signal of accumulating energy illusion.

2.3 Structural Classification

Criterion: net positive contribution to society's long-term forward motion (toward higher organizational levels), not short-term energy consumption.

  • Facilitative structures: raise the overall network's EROI; net positive contribution to long-term forward motion.
  • Destructive structures (e.g., warlords): organized; have an interest-binding with the survival of governed nodes; destruction is a means; have an inherent minimum floor of care.
  • Obstructive structures (嵌塞结构) (e.g., gentry, unique to post-Song China): embedded in socially necessary functional nodes; block all external complementary paths; energy output toward the outside approaches zero; maintain path monopoly through indispensability.
  • Corrosive-control structures (腐控结构) (unique to post-Song China, extreme form): embed in necessary functional nodes AND control the state apparatus AND reproduce themselves through civil-service exams and kin networks AND systematically exploit the regenerability of the base layer.
  • Dissipative structures: fully atomized, no organizational intent, randomly consuming residues after network hollowing.
  • Decayed state: the normal process of social disintegration — ordered structures spontaneously decompose, but the physical substrate remains, and decomposition products become the foundation for reconstruction.

2.4 Social Potential (社会势能)

Social potential is the collective term for the fixed forms of social energy — the organizational potential form and material crystallization form defined in 2.1. Social potential differs fundamentally from physical potential: physical potential is a neutral positional property; social potential is a cumulative historical property determined by the depth of consciousness-matter interaction motion accumulated in a structure. Social potential has gradations — the criterion is how much effective social energy the fixed structure can release when activated, and at what cost. High-grade social potential: deep accumulation (organizational capacity, technical knowledge, infrastructure) — low activation cost, high release. Low-grade: surface accumulation, internally hollowed — high activation cost, low effective release.

2.5 Potential Capacity and Gravity

Potential must exist in the form of organizations and structures — the human body cannot contain large quantities of potential. Two sources of gravity: conversion-efficiency gravity (natural gravity from higher energy conversion efficiency; low maintenance cost); path-control gravity (artificial gravity from path monopoly; high maintenance cost; negative contribution to network EROI).

2.6 Repulsion

Repulsion is a necessary condition for stable structures — a pure-gravity system without repulsion will inevitably collapse. Three sources: resource competition, boundary protection, frequency incompatibility.

2.7 Interaction Types: Four-Type Continuum

Any two social systems' interaction lies on a continuum — not in four discrete boxes. The four types describe tendencies:

  • Same-frequency, same-direction (Resonance): corresponds to constructive interference in wave theory; linear superposition; produces quantitative change; amplitude grows, frequency unchanged. Valid domain: individual and family levels directly; statistical effect at higher levels.
  • Same-frequency, opposite-direction (Resonance cancellation / Internal friction): frequency compatible but phase opposed; destructive interference; energy dissipates as waste heat. Social correspondent: factions with the same underlying structure but opposed directional commitments.
  • Different-frequency, compatible (Complementarity): corresponds to nonlinear frequency mixing; produces qualitatively new frequencies; genuine emergence. Source of technological breakthroughs and innovation.
  • Different-frequency, incompatible (Forced superposition): two entirely incompatible frequency structures forced into the same network; large-scale dissipation, no emergence. Colonial assimilation is a paradigm case.

2.8 Yun (韵) — The Central Object

Yun is the complete present state carried by a trajectory node — the indivisible whole left by consciousness and matter mutually shaping each other in motion, belonging to neither the consciousness side nor the matter side. Yun simultaneously exhibits particle-nature (discrete nodes with boundaries, positions, potential grades) and wave-nature (exhibiting frequency characteristics when interacting with other nodes); these two natures are inseparable.

Structurally, yun resembles a wave function — simultaneously encoding both particle and wave natures — but yun is a new mathematical object, not a social analogy of the wave function. The fundamental differences: under conscious action, yun does not collapse but continues to evolve and change its properties; conscious intervention is an internal constituent of yun, not an external observation. Measurement of yun can only be approximately estimated, not completely described — because the measurement act is itself part of the motion.

Lingering resonance (余韵) is yun's core characteristic: after the motion of consciousness-matter interaction passes through a node, the node is not a static remnant but a present form still vibrating.

2.9 PN Junction Pattern

An institutional structure within or between networks that constrains energy transmission to be unidirectional and asymmetric. Three modes of network-to-network interaction: bidirectional complementary exchange (positive-sum); PN junction unidirectional extraction (maintained automatically by institutional paths); forced reset (high-cost repulsion work). Rising PN junction density is the observable signal that path-control gravity is overwhelming conversion-efficiency gravity.

2.10 Flow vs. Stock

The two cannot substitute for each other: flow is a sustainable energy source; stock is a buffer when flow is interrupted, and the input basis for the next round of flow expansion. Human civilization has always been a consumer civilization — relying on the flow of ecosystem producers (photosynthesis) while simultaneously extracting stocks. Becoming producers (fusion energy) is the only path to escape the local minimum — not optimization but categorical change.

2.11 Natural Selection at the Social Level

Earth's physical constraints apply the same selection pressure to all social networks. Three conditions universally hold: variation, selection pressure, inheritance (through neural pathways, cultural products, material structures). Social-level natural selection speed is far faster than biological: cultural inheritance makes accumulation speed vastly exceed biological inheritance — processes that take tens of thousands of years biologically can complete in a few generations.

2.12 Inheritance Mechanism: Structural Fragment Survival

The unit of inheritance in social natural selection is structural fragments — the parts of a structure that have not yet disintegrated under collapse pressure. Three survival forms: material base remnants (infrastructure, roads, water systems — lifespans far exceed political structures); personnel carrying organizational capacity (taking division of labor, management knowledge, mobilization capacity into new environments for reactivation); cultural products (texts, institutional templates, technical records — longest temporal coverage). The mechanism by which resilience is generation-by-generation strengthened: every collapse's seed is the survivor of the previous collapse.

2.13 Degrees of Freedom

Degrees of freedom is the observable projection of conscious intervention capacity onto the material side — an analytical concept in the framework, not a measurement standard. Social-level correspondence: the number of independent paths a node or structure can choose within material constraint conditions. Nodes with high degrees of freedom can produce wider-ranging motion; nodes with low degrees of freedom have behavior constrained to near-determinacy. Operational use: when analyzing any social phenomenon, assess how much effective freedom exists and what is compressing it — path-control gravity reduces degrees of freedom; extractive structures reduce it; base-layer nodes' effective degrees of freedom approach zero in late-stage black holes. Relation to entropy: entropy is the framework's only measurement standard (quantifiable, judges energy flow direction); degrees of freedom is an ordinal analytical concept (judges whether consciousness can intervene in that direction).

2.14 Social Potential (社会势能)

Social potential is the collective term for the fixed forms of social energy — the organizational potential form and material crystallization form of 2.1. Flow is dynamic; social potential is static. Social potential differs fundamentally from physical potential: physical potential is a neutral positional property; social potential is a cumulative historical property determined by the depth of consciousness-matter interaction motion accumulated in a structure.

Two independent analytical dimensions: total quantity (scale and accumulation depth); grade (how much effective motion can be activated per unit social potential — yun accumulation quality and richness of complementary connections). Combination types: high quantity + high grade → strong conversion-efficiency gravity, voluntary aggregation; high quantity + low grade → primarily path-control gravity, forced aggregation; low quantity + high grade → small scale but quality exceeds same-scale low-grade structures; low quantity + low grade → gravity extremely weak. Black holes preferentially extract high-grade social potential (low activation cost, high release); what remains after extraction is low-grade — structures appear to exist but activate into nothing.

2.15 Translation Layer (翻译层)

Different societies' yun accumulates different historical trajectories. When two yun systems meet, they cannot directly resonate or complement because their frequency structures are incompatible — the default result is repulsion. A translation layer is an intermediate layer making cross-system interaction possible: simultaneously carrying partial frequency structures from both systems, transmitting between the two sides. Historical forms: border populations (paradigm: Sogdian merchants on the Silk Road); contact products (new things not fully belonging to either civilization); forced contact (war, colonialism — high repulsion, low translation efficiency, large dissipation). Cross-civilizational interaction efficiency depends not only on complementary potential but on the quality and thickness of the translation layer. Globalization's real problem is not homogenization itself but boundary disappearance degrading the conditions for generating new heterogeneity — the seed bank for new complementary potential is persistently depleted without replenishment.

2.16 Language and Writing

Language and writing are encoding tools for yun — compressing yun's frequency structure into low-entropy forms propagable across time and space. Without them, yun transmission is limited to emotional gravity's physical range. Language is real-time encoding (high information density, small range, non-durable). Writing is cross-temporal-spatial encoding (can cross time and space, but fixation loses information). Physical mechanism of cultural inheritance: descendants encounter texts and partially reconstruct original yun states. Translation is the most fundamental translation-layer form, but translation losses are structural: yun frequency information without corresponding vocabulary in the target language disappears; that with corresponding vocabulary but different semantic fields is distorted.

2.17 Potential Groups and Interest Groups

Potential group (势能集团): the cluster of nodes accumulating social potential by attaching to the same dominant structure — a dynamic yun-accumulation community, not a static positional description. When the dominant structure changes, potential groups reorganize. Interest group (利益集团): describes externally observable interest aggregation without involving internal yun mechanisms. The two are different observational depths of the same group. Both replace "class" within the framework, cutting off historical inevitability presuppositions and static positional meanings.

Complete definition requires two necessary layers: upper network (organizational capacity, institutional power, path-control gravity); base network (controls the material basis for energy extraction — the origin of stable social energy). Either missing renders the group incomplete. Whether a bureaucratic group becomes a potential group depends on controlling the base network — Chinese historical bureaucrats could because they held land and clan networks at home; modern career bureaucrats with only positions and no base network are strictly not potential groups. The real mechanism of penetrating the state apparatus: controlling the base network to influence the upper network's energy sources — if a potential group controls the base network and tax sources, the state apparatus is structurally dependent on it regardless of individual official loyalty.

2.18 The State Apparatus

The state apparatus is a tool and container with no independent interests. Tool: the means by which potential groups maintain and rewrite social networks; it produces no goals of its own. Container: provides an institutional framework within which different potential groups compete, coordinate, and move. The state apparatus is the central node of the upper network — highest connectivity, controlling main paths — which is why seizing it is every potential group's core goal.

Why no independent interests: the state apparatus exists only in the upper network, has no base network, no independent energy extraction basis; its operation depends on social energy input through taxation from potential groups. Elite-class stopping tax payment means withdrawal of the state apparatus's main energy source — not merely fiscal crisis. The degree of overlap between potential groups and the state apparatus is a continuum from pure tool-container independence to the corrosive-control extreme form. The state apparatus holds not all force but the right to define legitimacy: its own force is defined as legitimate; other structures' force is determined by it to be legitimate or not.

2.19 Three Forms of Coercive Force

Coercive force is the extreme form of structural repulsion. Three forms of the state apparatus's relationship to coercive force:

  • Monopoly type: legitimate coercive force fully concentrated in the state apparatus; others defined as illegal. Typical of capitalist society — commercial networks need unified predictable enforcement; unified monopoly reduces transaction costs. Consequence: capital groups lose direct coercive force and can only use it by controlling the state apparatus.
  • Aggregation type: the state apparatus's coercive force is the aggregate of commissioned coercive forces from major structures; structures withdrawing commission immediately collapses state coercive capacity. Typical of feudal and most pre-modern societies.
  • Competition type: the state apparatus and other structures simultaneously hold legitimate coercive force; boundaries blurred, continuously contested. Warlordism is the extreme — chronic attrition with no single revolutionary critical point.

Form determines revolutionary path: monopoly-type → must seize the state apparatus; aggregation-type → must get structures to withdraw commissioned force; competition-type → already in continuous boundary contestation.

2.20 Market

The market is an institutional network through which strangers coordinate social energy exchange using price signals — compressing yun differences into single numeric signals, enabling connections between nodes with no yun interaction history, supporting large-scale division-of-labor complementarity across emotional gravity boundaries. Division of labor + market simultaneously fully exploit resonance and complementarity: division of labor turns each node into a specialized resonance source; connections between specialized nodes are cross-frequency complementarity producing qualitative change; market enables these connections to cross boundaries. Without market, division of labor cannot exceed the effective range of emotional gravity.

Price as yun compression encoding: price flattens quality differences, production conditions, social relations, ecological impact, base-layer extraction intensity — all compressed away. The compressed information becomes market externalities; market selection pressure does not respond to information invisible to price signals. Market as social selection mechanism: structurally similar to natural selection (variation, selection pressure, inheritance all hold) but with soft boundaries — selection direction determined by institutional rules, theoretically modifiable through institutional change. This soft boundary means subjective agency has genuine operational space, unlike natural selection's hard physical boundaries. Capital not growing means dying: market competitive pressure compulsion, not a choice — extreme accumulation and expansion is the only survival strategy forcibly imposed through the market selection mechanism. Capital as social relation: a path-control gravity institutional form enabling nodes with existing social potential to automatically acquire priority extraction rights over newly produced social energy, encompassing the entire social environment of legal systems, state apparatus enforcement, and cultural ideology making this distribution natural.

2.21 Paths, Credit, and Contracts

Path: the fixed trace left by repeatedly occurring yun interactions. First interaction: high cost, uncertain. Repetition leaves traces, lowering future costs. Accumulated to a threshold: connection self-maintains, energy cost approaches minimum — a path is fixed. Paths are low-energy-cost interaction channels (principle of least action at the social level). Social energy cannot flow through empty space — it must be transmitted through existing paths. Paths require continuous work to maintain; stopping work causes spontaneous degradation.

Credit: expectation of a path's continuity — the prerequisite for investing social energy in any path. Credit is universal across all yun interaction paths, not only markets. Implementation mechanism varies from emotional gravity → reputation → institutional enforcement → legal contract as scale and strangeness increase. Credit collapse is path-expectation collapse. Economic crisis physical meaning: not total social energy decreasing, but credit basis collapse causing large-scale path rupture, immediately contracting effective social energy supply.

Contract: credit's institutionalized form in stranger networks — encoding exchange commitments into third-party-enforceable form (the state apparatus), enabling large-scale stranger credit relations without personal relationship dependence.

2.22 Culture, Ideology, and Morality

Culture, ideology, and morality are social products — yun, trajectory nodes of consciousness-matter interaction motion, not externally imposed. They reflect the common parts in the yun of the structures and nodes that produced them — products of resonance accumulation, naturally carrying the imprint of the material conditions of the historical stage that produced them.

Four main functions: (1) Reducing coordination costs — common culture means nodes don't rebuild trust from scratch each time; (2) Maintaining large-structure cohesion — beyond emotional gravity's range, ideology is the only tool maintaining cohesion across stranger networks; (3) Maintaining legitimacy of specific structures and paths — naturally maintains the path-control gravity of the structures that produced it; when those structures become destructive, it becomes an obstacle to new structures' movement space (same function, opposite effect); (4) Influencing the direction of nodes' motion — yun frequency structure weights certain paths as lower energy cost, others as higher, bidirectionally.

Large numbers of same-type structures produce resonance fields that spontaneously generate and reinforce matching culture — not designed, natural resonance product. Cultural generation positive feedback loop: dominant structure → same-type nodes → matching culture → lower energy cost for that structural form → reinforces dominant structure reproduction. Cultural lag: after dominant structure changes, the old culture has inertia — large numbers of nodes carrying old frequency structures need time to replace. Not human conservatism but the inertial effect of yun accumulation.

2.23 Three Classification Dimensions of the Social Network

Three independent dimensions must be explicitly distinguished:

Functional dimension (upper network / base network): stratified by social energy production and control relations. Base network directly extracts and produces social energy — the origin point. Upper network organizes, controls, and distributes energy flow direction — primary carrier of path-control gravity. Vertical functional stratification.

Scale dimension (individual → family → organization → group → society): stratified by the scale and cohesion mechanism of yun interaction. Each level has its own cohesion mechanism (emotional gravity → discipline-ideology → institutional rules), emergence mechanism, and inertia magnitude. Horizontal scale stratification.

The two dimensions are independent and intersect: same-scale nodes can be in either upper or base network; same functional level can span scales. Analysis must specify which dimension is in use — implicit switching is a methodological error.

Third dimension — exchange mechanism dimension (placeholder): describes the mechanism and direction of social energy exchange between nodes (market, planned allocation, gift economy, PN junction, etc.), division-of-labor structure, and connection directionality. Credit and contract are this dimension's infrastructure layer.

III. Physical Constraint Conditions

3.1 Basic Thermodynamic Constraints

  • Second law of thermodynamics: all positive consciousness-matter interactions are anti-entropic processes; they must consume available energy.
  • Carnot efficiency: the theoretical upper limit of heat engine efficiency; scientific optimization space is limited with diminishing marginal returns.
  • Landauer's principle: any irreversible information processing must consume energy; conscious activity has real physical costs.
  • Principle of least action: systems take the path of lowest energy cost. Blocking structures violate this principle and require conscious action to maintain — physical constraints will ultimately tighten again.

3.2 Two Distinct External Forces

The framework involves two fundamentally different types of external force that cannot be conflated: (1) inter-societal force ratios (e.g., imperialism vs. colony — subject to internal-cause constraint conditions); (2) thermodynamic boundary conditions (physical resistance of nature to extraction acts — the physical boundary conditions themselves are changing). No matter how strong internal potential, if external cause continuously compresses, the domain where internal cause dominates keeps narrowing.

3.3 Earth Energy Constraints

Earth receives approximately 1.74×10¹⁷ watts from the Sun; the ecosystem maintenance consumes over 99%; what is truly available for society is tiny. The ecosystem is the material prerequisite for social energy extraction — destruction on human civilizational timescales is irreversible. Fossil fuels are billions of years of compressed solar energy — a one-time stock liquidation, not a sustainable new energy acquisition mode.

Three-layer destruction by fossil fuel extraction: (1) Breaking the carbon cycle — releasing billions of years of fixed carbon into the atmosphere within centuries; (2) Altering energy flow structure — rewriting the boundary conditions under which current species distributions and ecosystems emerged; (3) Destroying the terrain accumulated by producers — soil organic matter, aquifer structures, geological stability — which are the material basis for producer recovery.

3.4 Physical Logic of External Expansion

When social networks face local-minimum pressure, they preferentially choose external expansion over internal extraction — external extraction consumes external stocks, while internal extraction destroys one's own physical substrate and resonance field. When external expansion fails (net returns below maintenance cost), system pressure shifts toward internal extraction, beginning to erode one's own physical substrate. Elite-class stopping support for top-level complexity is the earliest warning signal.

IV. Core Mechanisms

4.1 Organizational Emergence Circuit

Trigger conditions: material scarcity/survival crisis → node consciousness-matter interaction frequency convergence → resonance connections massively established → division of labor produces complementary connections → organizational EROI exceeds individual independent action → positive feedback established → potential accumulation → scale expansion.

Built-in destruction mechanism: power-law distribution is the mathematical inevitability of natural network growth; high-connectivity nodes automatically accumulate advantages and face internal pressure to shift from facilitative to extractive. After crisis resolution, resonance weakens and accumulated potential spontaneously shifts toward path control.

4.2 The Dynastic Cycle and Natural Selection

China's historical dynastic cycle is not merely circular but a process of continuous strengthening under natural selection pressure: each collapse is a natural selection pressure test; units that can maintain basic ordered states under minimal material conditions survive and transmit stronger organizational patterns to the next cycle.

4.3 Formation Conditions of Obstructive Structures (嵌塞结构)

Three conditions must simultaneously hold: land impoverished relative to population density (base-level nodes have near-zero surplus energy, unable to build alternative paths); geographic closure (reducing external interruption); functional vacuum created by clearing of old aristocracy (new structures fill the vacuum while deeply binding essential functions). Missing any one condition prevents formation. This is historically unique to post-Song China — cross-civilizational comparison with European feudal lords, Indian caste system, and Ottoman devshirme system confirms none simultaneously satisfy all three conditions.

4.4 Social Black Hole Theory

A social black hole is not a normal society but the terminal-stage syndrome of social disintegration — an energy extraction apparatus parasitic on social remnants.

Formation mechanism: a single structure eliminates conditions for complementary emergence → collective control requires homogenization → homogenization lowers structural carrying capacity → accumulated organizational potential exceeds the upper limit → collapse into a black hole.

Black hole's fundamental condition: all life-sustaining paths are under a single structure's control; no path exists outside the control range; nodes physically cannot leave this path network and survive. The unit system (单位制) is the clearest institutional manifestation: work, housing, medical care, children's education, food (ration coupons), social relations — all life-sustaining paths are inside the unit structure, with none outside its control.

Distinction from historical obstructive structures: traditional obstructive structures controlled some necessary paths, but base-level nodes retained self-sufficient space (land, sideline production, family production). The black hole eliminates this residual space — land reform removed private plots, market elimination removed independent exchange paths, the unit system collected all life-sustaining paths under unified control. This is the physical reason the black hole is more thoroughgoing than any historical obstructive structure, and why it appears for the first time in human history.

Reverse motion: The black hole is not passive degradation but driven reverse motion — actively decomposing high-organizational-level structures into lower organizational states, accelerating extraction of available energy into waste heat and the black hole's own gravitational potential energy. Two compounding positive feedbacks in late stage: (1) decreasing extractable stocks → higher gravitational pressure per unit stock → more direct and intense extraction → accelerating base-layer depletion; (2) base-layer depletion → material conditions for conscious-matter forward motion destroyed → resistance capacity decreases → reverse motion gains greater relative advantage. Late-stage self-acceleration results.

4.5 Four-Stage Development Sequence

  • Stage 1: Positive acceleration, kinetic accumulation, overall EROI rising.
  • Stage 2: Acceleration decreasing toward zero; inertia still driving forward motion; complexity gains real returns but with diminishing margins.
  • Stage 3: Overall acceleration turns negative, but accumulated kinetic energy (inertia) still drives forward motion. The system appears to be developing but net force is already reversed, consuming kinetic energy. The only time window for Stage 5 (fundamental energy source breakthrough) to appear.
  • Stage 4: Kinetic energy exhausted; sliding toward local minimum. Base-layer network material foundation monotonically degrades; each cycle takes away irrecoverable portions.

Inter-stage critical point: average EROI of extractable stocks falls below the maintenance cost of extraction capacity — earlier than any observable social signal.

4.6 Characterization of Socialism

Not a death rattle, but the last large-scale attempt at forward motion — the inertia of thousands of years of consciousness-matter interaction motion, released through phase transition under extreme conditions where the material substrate was nearly exhausted and all normal paths were sealed.

Moving toward a black hole was highly probable: physical substrate overdrawn + destroying the base network was the necessary price + single structure eliminated conditions for complementary emergence + homogenization lowered the carrying capacity limit — four factors compounding. Not anyone's mistake, but the physical constraints of consumer civilization under conditions of extreme substrate exhaustion setting the endpoint of this path.

Socialism shattered the old network — completely unlike externally catalyzed new structures (Africa, Asia, Latin America). External catalysis: old inertia field too strong, new structures dragged, cannot reach design limits. Socialism: old inertia field massively destroyed, new structures lost the base network's resilience buffer, organizational potential exceeded carrying capacity limit and collapsed.
